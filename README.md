# Smart-City-Bike-Sharing-Data-in-PowerBi

## Smart City Bike Sharing Data Analysis

## Overview
This analysis evaluates bike-sharing operations across cities using key metrics such as revenue per km, utilization, and cost efficiency. Since revenue and distance were not directly available, they were derived using business assumptions to simulate real-world performance.


## Key Insights
## 1.	Revenue per KM (City Performance)
•	Top Performing Cities: Cities with high bike usage and balanced station distribution show higher revenue per km, indicating efficient utilization. 
•	Lowest Performing Cities: Cities with frequent empty/full stations generate lower revenue per km, showing operational imbalance. 
Balanced availability of bikes and docks directly improves revenue efficiency.



## 2.	Underperforming Cities (Low-Cost Efficiency)
•	Cities with: 
o	High operational cost (more bike stands, low usage) 
o	Low ride activity 
These cities are cost-heavy but underutilized.

## 3.	Station - Level Issues
•	Many stations fall into: 
o	Empty → Lost revenue opportunity 
o	Full → No docking space → customer dissatisfaction 
Poor redistribution leads to inefficiency.


## Recommendations
## 1. Improve Bike Redistribution
•	Move bikes from low-demand to high-demand stations 
•	Reduce empty/full station scenarios 

## 2. Optimize Underperforming Cities
•	Reduce excess capacity (bike stands) 
•	Reallocate bikes to high-performing cities 

## 3. Time-Based Strategy
•	Increase bike availability during peak hours 
•	Reduce idle inventory during off-peak

## 4. Improve Cost Efficiency
•	Focus on cities with high revenue per km 
•	Reassess operations in low-efficiency cities 

## 5. Use Real-Time Monitoring
•	Implement alerts for: 
o	Empty stations 
o	Full stations 
•	Enable dynamic decision-making

## Conclusion 
The analysis highlights that efficient bike distribution, time-based demand planning, and city-level optimization are key to improving revenue and operational efficiency in bike-sharing systems.
